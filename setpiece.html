Key Principles

Wall Principles

  • GK is ALWAYS the primary organizer — field players respond immediately
  • Tallest players in the center of the wall, decreasing height toward the edges
  • Wall alignment: ball → near post — blocks the most direct shot
  • The player in front of the ball WALKS BACKWARD — do not sprint or turn; buy time
  • Once GK calls 'WALL SET' — hold absolutely still. No peeking.

TRANSITION AFTER SET PIECES

Every set piece ends with a transition. Be ready BEFORE the set piece ends — ball-watching is where goals are conceded.

✓ If We Win Possession

  • Play quickly — attack before they recover shape
  • #11: hold width, be a threat in behind
  • #2 and #4: push up and support the attack
  • First pass: away from pressure, into space
  • If it breaks wide, attack the channel immediately

✗ If We Lose Possession

  • Immediately get goal-side — do not chase the ball
  • Players in box: sprint back into defensive shape
  • Defensive players: press the counter immediately
  • GK: communicate — call 'COUNTER' so everyone reacts
  • Recover to shape — don't ball-watch

The Golden Rule

  • The moment of transition is when teams are most vulnerable — be mentally ready before the set piece ends
  • The first 3 seconds after a set piece are the most dangerous — reaction speed wins or loses games
  • Communicate immediately — everyone must know whether we are attacking or defending
